About Me

Hello! My name is Marybeth Hamilton, and I just finished my 17th year as a music educator. I currently serve as the choral director at McNabb Middle School in Montgomery County (Mt. Sterling), where I lead the choral program and direct music for the McNabb Musical Theater Program. In addition to my school role, I work as a clinician, adjudicator, and soloist for events across the state. I am also an active performer, singing year round with the Louisville Chamber Choir. I have recently appeared as the alto soloist in Mozart’s Requiem and Handel's Messiah, both with the Grammy Award winning Louisville Orchestra. I currently live in Winchester, Kentucky, with my husband, who is also a choral director, and our children, Liam and Caroline.

Education & School Librarianship

I earned both my Bachelor of Music Education and Bachelor of Music in Vocal Performance degrees from the University of Louisville in 2008, and later completed my Master’s in Education from the University of the Cumberlands in 2014. I am currently completing coursework toward the Rank I School Librarian Program at Eastern Kentucky University . Outside of music, reading has always been a deeply personal passion of mine. During my Master’s degree program in Reading and Writing Intervention, I took a children’s literature course that became my favorite part of the program and sparked my interest in becoming a school librarian. The library has always been my happy place, and I am excited to help create that same sense of belonging for all students.

Professional Development and Collaboration

In addition to my work in education, I am available as a clinician and presenter for professional development opportunities. I enjoy collaborating with educators, librarians, and school leaders to support student learning and foster a culture of literacy and lifelong learning. My professional development sessions are designed to be practical, engaging, and immediately applicable in educational settings. I strive to provide participants with strategies, resources, and ideas they can take back to their classrooms, libraries, and schools.

What can you find on my website?

Throughout this website, you can find information about professional development and collaboration opportunities, my book recommendations for students, a link to my blog, resources for teachers, and my contact information. Whether you are an educator looking for instructional support, a reader searching for your next great book, or a colleague interested in collaborating, I hope you find these resources helpful and informative.
